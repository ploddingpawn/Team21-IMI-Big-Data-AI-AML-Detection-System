AML Typology Feature Reference
Isolation Forest — TYPOLOGY_FEATURE_PATTERNS
What each feature pattern is capturing, mapped to AML-indicator-DB.xlsx

Overview
The typology-specific Isolation Forest ensemble trains one unsupervised model per AML typology.
Each model receives a curated feature set — the TYPOLOGY_FEATURE_PATTERNS — derived
directly from the AML-indicator-DB.xlsx indicator catalogue. This document explains what each
feature is measuring and which AML indicator it operationalises.
Five typologies are modelled:
• 1. Structuring & Layering — threshold avoidance and rapid fund velocity
• 2. Behavioural & Profile Anomalies — activity inconsistent with KYC profile
• 3. Trade-Based ML & Shell Entities — business volume far exceeding declared activity
• 4. Cross-Border & Geographic Risk — transactions with high-risk jurisdictions
• 5. Human Trafficking — MCC-level card and transfer patterns linked to exploitation

1. Structuring & Layering
This typology targets deliberate avoidance of currency-reporting thresholds (STRUCT-001..009)
combined with rapid pass-through of funds (ATYPICAL-006..008). The two previously separate
typologies — Structuring and Layering — are merged here because both share the same core
signals: unusual transaction sizing and high fund velocity.

2. Behavioural & Profile Anomalies
This typology captures activity that is inconsistent with what would be expected given a customer's
declared KYC profile — occupation, income, account history, and prior behaviour. It consolidates

the former money-mule and profile-mismatch typologies, since both target PROF and ACCT
indicators.

2.1 Spending vs Income Mismatch
Living beyond one's means is a defining AML indicator (PROF-002, PROF-005). These features
measure the gap between what flows out and what the customer's profile would predict as income.
Feature Name

AML Indicator

What It Captures

spending_to_income_ratio

PROF-002/005

Total debits divided by estimated or declared income.
Values significantly above 1.0 indicate spending that
cannot be explained by legitimate income.

income_vol_ratio

PROF-001

Transaction volume relative to expected account
activity established at onboarding. Captures accounts
that dramatically exceed their projected usage.

2.2 Sudden Changes in Activity
Abrupt shifts in transaction patterns — not just high volume — are the key PROF-010 and ACCT004 signals. A previously quiet account that suddenly processes large flows is anomalous even if
the absolute values are not extreme.

std_transaction_amount

PROF-008

Standard deviation of transaction amounts in absolute

3. Trade-Based ML & Shell Entities
This typology targets businesses whose financial flows are inconsistent with their declared
commercial activity (PML-TBML-02..08, PROF-004). It also captures gatekeeper pass-through via
GATE-001 — accounts used by professionals (lawyers, accountants, realtors) as conduits for client
funds.

3.1 Volume vs Declared Business Activity
The foundational trade-based ML signal: a business transacts far more than its declared sales
revenue or industry norms would suggest.
Feature Name

AML Indicator

What It Captures

volume_to_sales_ratio

PROF-004/PMLTBML-05

Total transaction volume divided by the customer's
declared annual sales. A ratio well above 1 means the
business is moving more money than it claims to earn
— the core TBML signal.

inflow_per_day

PML-TBML-04

Average daily inflow. Sudden large inflows that are
inconsistent with the business's normal operating
rhythm flag the PML-TBML-04 sudden-spike indicator.

total_inflow

PML-TBML-04

Cumulative inflow over the observation window. High
totals relative to declared business size confirm the
volume mismatch.

terrorist financing risk (GEO-001..005) and wire transfer patterns inconsistent with the customer's
financial profile (WIRE-008/010).

spending patterns associated with sexual exploitation.
